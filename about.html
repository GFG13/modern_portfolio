About Me

Greg Gambino

With a passion for analytical problem-solving, I've managed a complex research project, proposed novel rock testing techniques, and communicated my work on an international stage. As a graduate student leader, I've been an advocate for life-long learning and encourage ongoing personal and professional development.

University of Toronto

Researcher (PhD. Candidate)

Throughout my academic career, I've investigated problems related to rock strength and mechanical stability of excavations, while also having exposure to state-of-the-art methods for characterising stress uncertainty using statistical tools. As part of my PhD, I carried out experimental testing on thawing ice-filled rock fractures, developed a multi-physics numerical simulation to model the experiment, and analysed the effect of thawing permafrost by measuring underground temperatures in a full-scale mine drift in Siberia, Russia.

Undergraduate Course Instructor

Blasting & Fragmentation in Mining; Rock Mechanics; Geology Field Mapping

I prepared and facilitated engaging lectures and tutorials, and undertook robust assessments for mineral engineering coursework. I've also been involved in implementing a flipped-classroom teaching strategy, in an effort to increase student engagement. I continually aim to provide students with experiential learning opportunities, such as a site visit to witness explosions at a local quarry, with whom I've been able to establish a long-standing relationship.

ILead:Grad

Chair of Executive Comittee

My role as chair allowed me to exercise my leadership skills through facilitating regular meetings as well as planning and executing team-building events and educational workshops for UofT graduate students aimed at different aspects of leadership development. During my time with ILead:Grad, we managed to increased student attendance of events by 20% compared to the previous year, for a total of 150+ attendees at a single event, fulfilling our organization's purpose and aligning with our strategic direction.

Creator and Lead Organizer

Graduate Student Seminar Series

While completing my master's degree, I noticed a severe lack of comradery, and communication amongst my peers in my department. In response, I pioneered a collaborative graduate student forum to share knowledge and collect feedback on research and presentation skills. I successfully consulted department administrators to gain support and recruited industry professionals for several keynote presentations.

Supervisor

XVII PanAm Games

Shortly after finishing my competitive swimming career, I took on a supervisory volunteering role with Swimming Canada to eventually manage and train a large group of youth volunteers to effectively assist world-class swimmers at the 2015 PanAm Games. This involved directing and coordinating our youth volunteers during the swimming competition and the medal ceremonies.

Athlete

Swimmer, Triathlete

I've spent over 15 years as competitive swimmer with National-level success, with some time as varsity team captain (2011-2012) at the University of Waterloo. More recently, I've been putting my energy towards training for triathlons.
